Trademark Guides

Navigate the complex legal stages of international intellectual property protection.

Understanding the proper legal category of your intellectual property is essential before beginning any registry check. Filing under the wrong classification leads to automatic refusal.

Asset Type Legal Shield Protects... Example
Trademark Aegis Shield Registry Brand names, logos, slogans, acoustic signifiers "Google", logo icons
Copyright Statutory Author Shield Original creative creations (novels, software code, art) Source code repository, music
Patent Industrial Utility Shield Novel functional mechanisms, designs, drug formulations Touchscreen hardware mechanism

The standard government registration process is highly structured and typically requires several months to complete. Below is the chronological pipeline:

Phase 1: Search & Audit (Week 1)

Execute availability scans using advanced phonetic query engines. Resolve potential similarity objections beforehand.

Phase 2: Registry Submission (Week 2)

Draft standard legal templates and assign Nice Classification codes. Submit documents to federal or WIPO offices.

Phase 3: Legal Examination (Months 1-3)

Government lawyers review the trademark for generic terms, distinctiveness, and registry duplication conflicts.

Phase 4: Publication & Opposition (Months 4-6)

The mark is published in the official IP Gazette. Third parties have 60-90 days to log disputes/objections.

The Nice Agreement divides goods and services into 45 distinct classes. You must choose classes carefully—once submitted, you cannot add classes to an existing application.

  • Review Product Verticals: Map your core product functions. If you sell e-commerce software and also ship retail physical apparel, you must file under both Class 42 (software services) and Class 25 (apparel).
  • Assess Future Features: If your startup plans to integrate crypto payment gates next year, register under Class 36 (financial systems) during the initial phase.
  • Avoid Broad Descriptors: Vague class claims will cause examiners to issue immediate office action objections requesting revisions.

Compliance Checklist

Ensure your corporate brand parameters comply with legal requirements before registry submission.

Name Distinctiveness

Ensure the mark is not descriptive or generic (e.g. cannot register "The Laptop Company").

Phonetic Scan Clearance

Ensure spelling variations don't mimic existing trademark acoustical sounds.

Geographical Neutrality

The mark must not claim geographic exclusivity without unique distinction.
