A detailed roadmap detailing international filing classifications, legal thresholds, and administrative requirements.

Understanding the Registration Process

Securing a trademark registry entry involves navigating multi-jurisdiction laws, Nice Class classifications, and proof of use files. At Aegis Mark, our attorneys assume total operational control of this process, translating governmental requirements into straightforward steps.

Key Registration Criteria

To successfully clear examination at federal and WIPO registries, a trademark must satisfy three essential factors:

1. High Distinctiveness

Generic terms or descriptive traits cannot be registered. The name must be unique and represent a proprietary source indicator.

2. Non-Similarity

It must avoid likelihood of confusion with currently active filings in identical or related commercial classes.

Understanding the Nice Classification

Federal and international filings require assigning your trademark to specific classes representing your trade activities. There are 45 distinct classes (1-34 for goods, 35-45 for services). Selecting the wrong class leaves key areas undefended, or results in immediate refusal.

  • Goods Classes (e.g. Class 09): Protects tangible products like computers, microchips, software apps, digital files, and consumer items.
  • Service Classes (e.g. Class 35 & 42): Protects corporate operations, advertising portals, SaaS tools, and expert legal advisories.
