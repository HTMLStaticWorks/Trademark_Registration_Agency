General IP Questions

A trademark is a unique signifier (which can be a business name, logo phrase, slogan, design parameter, or combination thereof) used by an enterprise to distinguish its products or services from competing entities in commerce.

Standard trademark registrations last for 10 years from the initial filing date. Registrations can be maintained indefinitely, provided you submit proof of use declarations and renewal fees within the designated window (typically every 10 years).

Registration & Filing

The Madrid Protocol is an international treaty that enables brand owners to secure trademark protection in up to 130 countries by submitting a single application template to their home office, eliminating multiple cross-border attorney fees.
